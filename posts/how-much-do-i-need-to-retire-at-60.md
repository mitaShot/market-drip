Calculating your retirement number is the first step to financial freedom. Many experts suggest the 4% rule, but does it still hold up in 2025?

## The 4% Rule Explained

Traditionally, if you withdraw 4% of your portfolio in the first year of retirement and adjust for inflation thereafter, your money should last 30 years.

## Adjusting for 60

Retiring at 60 means you need your money to last longer than if you retired at 65. You might need to withdraw only 3.5% or save a larger nest egg.
